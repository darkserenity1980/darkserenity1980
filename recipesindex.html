Here are three Banana Recipes that I have tried, that were really quick and easy to make!

  • Banana Oatmeal Cookies
  • Banana and Blueberry Smoothie

Banana Oatmeal Cookies

This is what you will need:

  • 2 super ripe Bananas
  • 1 cup of oats
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips (this recipe used raisins but I like chocolate!)
  1. To start, preheat your oven to 350 degrees F and grease your baking utensil you will be using to make these cookies.
  2. In a decently sized bowl, add your bananas and mash it with a fork until smooth.
  3. Then add your oats, cinnamon, vanila, and chocolates and mix well with the mashed bananas.
  4. Using a regular sized spoon, scoop a good amount of your dough and add to your baking utensil.
  5. Then flatten the dough by using the back of the spoon or other item of choice to flatten the dough.
  6. Bake for 10 to 15 minutes and then let it cool after removing them out of the oven before serving!

Banana and Blueberry Smoothie

This is what you will need:

  • A blender
  • 1 Banana (somewhat ripe to very ripe is okay)
  • 1 cup of Blueberries
  • 1/2 cup of Coconut Milk (I usually use Soymilk)
  • 1 tbsp of Honey
  • 1 tbsp of Lime Juice
  • 1/4 tsp of Almond Extract (I usually skip this ingredient)
  • 1 cup of Ice
  1. This is a pretty easy recipe! You just have to add your banana, blueberries, your milk preference, honey, lime juice, almond extract, and ice to the blender and blend then enjoy!

Banana Bread

This is what you will need:

  • 2 & 1/3 mashed overripe Bananas
  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp of baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp of salt
  • 3/4 cup of brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup of warm butter
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  1. To start, preheat your oven to 350 degrees F and grease your baking utensil you will be using to make your banana bread.
  2. To a large bowl, add flour, baking soda, and salt and mix. Then in a separate large bowl, add brown sugar and butter and use an electric mixer (or you can hand mix yourself) until smooth.
  3. To the brown sugar and butter bowl, add your eggs and mashed bananas and stir until it is well blended.
  4. Then combine your dry mixture to the wet mixture and mix well.
  5. Next, you pour your banana batter into your baking utensil.
  6. Then bake in the oven for about 60 minutes.
  7. After baking, let the bread cool for 10 minutes and then serve.
